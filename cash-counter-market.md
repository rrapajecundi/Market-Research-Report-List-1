<p><p>Cash Counter market types refer to mechanisms for handling cash transactions in retail and trading environments. In a Single Pocket market, cash transactions are executed in one designated area, simplifying operations and tracking but limiting efficiency. Conversely, a Double Pocket market allows transactions to occur simultaneously in two separate areas, enhancing speed and capacity to manage higher volumes. Both types serve to streamline cash handling, but the Double Pocket model often offers improved flexibility and customer service due to its dual processing capability.</p></p>
